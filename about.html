About our SEPEP program

What is SEPEP?

SEPEP stands for Sports Education in Physical Education Program.

SEPEP is constructed around a student centred approach to physical education. This is where the teachers step down from the 'leaders' of the class and allow their students to take control and collaborate on the roles and responsibilities involved.

The SEPEP program provides an experience of what the real world will be like, in their jobs and everyday lives.

Roles

In each team, every group member is given a special role. There are six of these roles, and each one focuses on different aspects that bring the SEPEP program together.


Team Captain

The Team Captain is incharge of various duties to make sure that their team is performing at their best and keeping up with their tasks. The Team Captain works closely with the Coach to find the strengths and weaknesses of each player and find which combination of players works best together in a game. They are also incharge of encouraging and supporting team members to make sure everyone feels included.


Coach

The Coach is incharge of coaching the team so that they perform at their best during a game. They are incharge of coming up with drills and warm-ups to do at the start of each training session. The Coach works closely with the Team Captain to fing the strengths and weaknesses of each player and find which combination of players works best together in a game. They are also incharge of coming up with strategies to implent in a competitive game.


Governing Body

The Governing Body in each group has worked with other Governing Bodies to come up with and modify the rules of the game. They have the most knowledge of the rules and how the game should be played. They are incharge of clarlifying the rules to team members to ensure that everyone knows the rules so they do not lose any points during a competitive game.


Results Coordinator

The Results Coordinator is incharge of scoring games to make sure that all scores are accurate. The Results Coordinator are working closely with their Governing Body and deciding on what an efficient and accurate score sheet should look like for competitive matches. They are responsible for creating fixtures or schedule for upcoming matches, which can be found here. They have also worked with Media/Publicity by taking photos during practice sessions or matches that then are uploaded onto the website Gallery


Equipment Officer

The Equipment Officer is incharge of all of the equiment their group uses. They have to keep track of everything that their group needs or is using. On a rare occasion, they must report any damage or missing equipment to their teacher. At the end of each practical session, they are incharge of properly sanitizing all of the equipment to comply with the Covid-19 guidelines.


Media/Publicity

The Media/Publicity person is incharge of all things to do with media. Mainly they manage and maintain this website to keep up-to-date with any matches. They are responsible for communicating with the Result Coordinators to ensure that the scores of the matches are accurate and updated fairly often. They also must take photos of people participating in the sport during practice sessions or competitive games. After each match, they are also responsible for writing up a brief match report which is uploaded to the website for anyone to read.


Spikeball

The sport we are given to play during this SEPEP unit is Spikeball.
Spikeball is a net sport inspired primarily by concepts from volleyball. There is a circular net in the middle of the players on which the ball bounces as it is passed to the different teams. The aim of the game is to successfully bounce the ball on the net to the other team and for them to not be able to hit it back.


Rules

These are the official (and slightly modified) rules that our Governing Body has come up with. These are the rules that are followed during each competitive game of Spikeball.


  1. Spikeball is a no contact sport. If deliberate contact is made (decided by umpire), then replay the point.
  2. The games are 8 minutes each, split into two halves for subbing. You MUST sub at least one player when the first half is over.
  3. If timeout is needed (eg. Due to injury or subbing in other players), make a “T” to indicate this. Timeouts last 30 seconds maximum, unless otherwise specified.
  4. Games are won by which team has the most points at the end of the 8 minutes.
  5. No guarding or touching the net with any part of the body. This rule also includes obstruction of gameplay & players. If this occurs, replay the point.
  6. Everyone must have a turn at serving – you must not have the same server more than three times in a row.
  7. The team which won the last point serves.
  8. When serving, there are 2 faults (like in tennis) before possession changes and a point is given to the other team.
  9. Points are scored if:
    1. The other team fails to return the ball to the net
    2. The team cannot return the ball to the net on the third pass
    3. Any contact from a player is made with the net
    4. The ball hits the rim when bounced on the net
    5. The other team touched the ball without it hitting the net
  10. All body parts can be used to hit the ball, however you must serve by throwing it at the net with your hands, otherwise it will be counted as a fool.
  11. Ball must bounce out of the net to still be in play.
  12. There is a ‘halfway line’ during a serve only, which horizontally divides the playing space while a team serves; once the ball bounces off the net, the play is 360 degrees.
  13. Ball cannot go higher/further than 2 meters from the net.
